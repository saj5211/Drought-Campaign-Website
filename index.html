Welcome to the Water The Earth Project!

Water The Earth, is a project focused on finding innovative ways of creating water so the number of droughts annually and the impacts associated with these droughts are reduced.

What Are Droughts?

To keep things simple, Droughts are periods of time in which different places in the world experiences a deficiency of water. Droughts are often characterized by these three types:

Meteorological Droughts

Occurs when there is a deficiency of perciptation in a specific region. Regions like California often experience this type of drought.

Agricultural Drought

Occurs when there is a low level of topsoil moisture. This type of drought usually occur inconjunction with Meteorological Droughts or Hydrological Droughts.

Hydrological Droughts

Occurs when there is a decrease in water availability. This usually occurs in places where the level of infastructure is fairly limited.

How are Droughts Formed?

  • High Pressure (Affects Meteorological & Agricultural Droughts)
    • The sinking motion of air results in compressional warming which prevents cloud formations and results in lower relative humdity
    • Overall increases in global temperatures have led to an increase in High Pressure
  • Global Weather Patterns (Affects Meteorological & Agricultural Droughts)
    • El Niño: An anomaly where temperatures around the Pacific Region are higher than normal resulting in widespread droughts across the west coast of the United States.
  • Environmental Changes (Affects Hydrological & Agricultural Droughts)
    • Bodies of water dry up
    • Rivers dry up
  • Limitations In Infastructure (Affects Hydrological Droughts & Agricultural Droughts)
    • Not enough water is produced
    • Water pipes are not maintained

Solutions to Droughts

Though there is no real solution to preventing droughts. The following three solution hold the potential to being used one day to prevent a drought.

Fog Catchers

A device used to trap water droplets from fog. This device is mainly placed on top of hills or high places where the density of fog is at its highest.

Cloud Seeding

A solution where a plane drops crystals in the air that promote the formation of clouds resulting in percipitation.

Learn More
Waste Water Recycling

A solution where a water plant takes in wastewater and filters it until the water is safe enough to drink.

What You Can Do

There is a lot you can do to help prevent future droughts and reduce the impact it has on you and the people around you.

  • Reduce Your Water Usage

    Reducing your water usage allows water to be distributed to more people and puts less strain on infastructure

  • Pollute Less

    Polluting less allows for less heat to get trapped in the Earth's atmosphere which discourages the fomration of high pressure therefore reducing the frequency and longevity of droughts.

  • Donate to Charities, Projects, or Foundations

    Donating to these organizations allows them to further expand their operation and provide the support people need in times of droughts.
